How You Get Paid

The Money

How Commission Actually Works

  • Every carrier we write — except Ethos and Aetna — pays a 9-month advance: 75% of your first-year commission, paid up front when the client's first premium drafts.
  • Carriers apply comp hits ranging from 0–15% for immediate coverage products. For graded or guaranteed issue products, comp hits can run 50% or more.
Worked example

You write a $1,000 annual premium policy at a 100% contract. Your advance is 75% of the first-year commission — so $750. With a 0% comp hit, that full $750 hits your bank when the first premium drafts. With a 5% comp hit, it's $750 minus 5%.

  • Annual premium, at a 100% contract $1,000.00
  • Advance — 75%, paid on first draft $750.00
  • With a 0% comp hit, you're paid $750.00
  • With a 5% comp hit, you're paid $712.50

Chargebacks — The Flip Side of the Advance

That 75% advance is the carrier paying you up front on premium the client hasn't paid yet. If the client cancels or stops paying inside the advance period, the unearned portion gets charged back — the carrier takes it back from your future commissions. This is why the sale isn't done at the application: write quality business, set honest expectations, get them drafting, and keep your clients on the books.
